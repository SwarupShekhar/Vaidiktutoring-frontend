Calculator Setup & 10 Traps

GCSE Maths Paper 3 (Calculator) · Wednesday 10 June 2026

Casio is the most common GCSE calculator (fx-83/85 GT and fx-991 ClassWiz). The keys below match those models — if yours differs, the menu names are the same. Do this 60-second setup before you start the paper.

⚙️ 60-Second Setup (do this first)

  • Reset to default: SHIFT 9 3 = AC. Clears any leftover STAT/TABLE mode from a previous user.
  • Check it says DEGREES: look for a small D at the top of the screen. If you see R (radians) every trig answer will be wrong. Fix: SHIFT MODE → 3 (Deg).
  • Natural display on: SHIFT MODE → 1 (MathIO) so fractions and surds show properly. Use S↔D to flip any answer to a decimal.

🎯 The 10 Marks-Losing Traps

  1. Radians instead of degrees. The single most common Paper 3 error. No D on screen → every sin/cos/tan answer is wrong. Fix: check for the D before question 1.
  2. Squaring a negative. The calculator does the power before the minus, so −3² = −9 but (−3)² = 9. Always wrap a negative in brackets before squaring.
  3. Rounding too early. Round only the final answer. Keep the full value on screen and use Ans for the next step, or store it: SHIFT STO A. Early rounding loses the accuracy mark.
  4. Fractions over a sum. Typing 1 ÷ 2 + x computes (1÷2)+x. For a fraction over a whole expression, bracket the bottom: 1 ÷ (2 + x).
  5. The quadratic / cosine-rule denominator. In the cosine rule and quadratic formula, put the whole top and the whole bottom in brackets, e.g. (−b + √(b²−4ac)) ÷ (2a).
  6. Standard form. Enter powers of ten with the ×10ˣ (or EXP) button, not by typing × 10 ^. Typing 3 ×10ˣ 8 gives 3×10⁸.
  7. Wrong answer format. If a question says "give your answer to 2 decimal places" or "as a percentage", a fraction or surd on screen scores 0. Press S↔D to convert, then round as asked.
  8. Inverse trig for angles. To find an angle use SHIFT then sin/cos/tan (sin⁻¹). Forgetting SHIFT gives you a side ratio, not the angle.
  9. Iteration without Ans. Type the formula once using Ans where xₙ appears, press =, then keep pressing = — each press is the next iteration. Free marks if you know this.
  10. Trusting the screen over the working. "Show that" and method-mark questions need every line written down. The calculator gives the answer; the examiner pays for the working. Write it out even when the number is obvious.
