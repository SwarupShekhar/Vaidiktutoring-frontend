The Ultimate Topic Prediction Hit-List

Important: This hit-list is generated by analyzing topics that have already appeared on Paper 1 and Paper 2. While these topics have a very high statistical probability of appearing on Paper 3, you should still ensure you have a broad understanding of the entire syllabus.

After a rigorous analysis of Paper 1 and Paper 2, our mathematics department has identified major topics that Edexcel has not yet tested. Because these topics are core to the syllabus, their absence in the first two papers means they are incredibly likely to appear in Paper 3 (Calculator).

🔥 Tier 1: Extremely High Probability (Must Know)

These topics almost always appear across the three papers. Since they haven't shown up yet, prioritize these tonight:

  • Trigonometry (3D and Advanced): Sine rule, Cosine rule, and area of a triangle (½ab sinC). Expect a 4-5 mark applied question.
  • Advanced Statistics & Histograms: Estimating the mean from grouped frequency tables, drawing or interpreting histograms (remember: frequency density = frequency ÷ class width).
  • Quadratic Inequalities & Simultaneous Equations: Solving simultaneous equations where one is linear and one is quadratic.
  • Vectors: Vector proofs and geometry. Usually the last or second-to-last question on the paper.

📈 Tier 2: Medium-High Probability (Calculator Heavy)

These topics rely heavily on calculator use and are perfect for Paper 3:

  • Compound Interest & Depreciation: Using multipliers (e.g., 1.05^n for 5% increase over n years). Look out for questions asking you to find the number of years.
  • Bounds and Error Intervals: Calculating upper and lower bounds in complex calculations (e.g., maximum speed = upper bound distance ÷ lower bound time).
  • Iteration: Using iterative formulas to find approximate solutions to equations. This is guaranteed easy marks if you know how to use the 'ANS' button on your calculator!
  • Circle Theorems: Often combined with trigonometry or algebra. Make sure you can state the reason clearly (e.g., "Alternate segment theorem").

🎯 Top 3 Examiner Tricks to Watch Out For

  • Units mismatch: They will give you dimensions in cm but ask for volume in litres. (Remember: 1000cm³ = 1 Litre).
  • Rounding too early: Only round your final answer. Keep the full number on your calculator display during intermediate steps, otherwise you will lose the final accuracy mark.
  • "Show that..." questions: You must write out every single line of working. You cannot jump steps, even if you do it in your head or on the calculator.
